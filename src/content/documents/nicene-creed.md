The Nicene Creed is the church's conciliar response to the great
fourth-century debates over the person of Christ and the Holy Spirit.
Drafted at the Council of Nicaea in 325, expanded at the Council of
Constantinople in 381, and received in the Western liturgical tradition
with the addition of the *filioque* — *and the Son* — by the early
eleventh century, it is the single creed shared (with that one
contested clause) by Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican, Lutheran, Reformed,
and Methodist churches. Where the Apostles' Creed is the baptismal
spine of the West, the Nicene is the eucharistic confession of the
whole Church.

the Western Latin received form; the canonical English shown on the
